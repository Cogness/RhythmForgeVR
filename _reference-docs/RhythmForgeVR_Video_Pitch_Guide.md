## FULL PRODUCTION GUIDE

---

### A. WHAT YOU NEED TO RECORD

You have three types of footage to produce:

| Type | Source | Shots Needed |
|------|--------|-------------|
| **Real VR footage** | Quest 3 screen capture of your working prototype | Beats 3 & 4 (the core demos) |
| **Real camera footage** | Phone or webcam, you in a clean room | Beats 2 & 6 (putting on/removing headset) |
| **AI-generated footage** | AI video tool from a prompt or reference image | Beats 1 & 5 (concept shots, the "galaxy" hero shot) |

---

### B. RECORDING YOUR QUEST 3 FOOTAGE

This is the most important footage — it proves the concept is real.

**How to capture:**

1. **Meta Quest built-in recording:** From inside the headset, press the Meta button → select "Record Video." This captures what you see at 1080p. Good enough for a 1-minute pitch.
2. **SideQuest / scrcpy (better quality):** Connect Quest 3 to your computer via USB-C. Use [scrcpy](https://github.com/Genymobile/scrcpy) (free, open-source) to mirror and record the Quest display on your Mac at higher quality. This gives you a clean MP4 file to work with.
3. **Meta Quest Developer Hub (MQHD):** Free official tool from Meta. Connect via USB or Wi-Fi, use the "Cast" feature to mirror to your computer, and screen-record on your Mac using QuickTime or OBS.

**What to capture (do multiple takes of each):**

- **Take 1: Draw the rhythm circle.** Start with empty space. Draw a clean circle. Pause briefly so the viewer can see the completed shape.
- **Take 2: Place trigger points.** Tap 4 points on the circle with the stylus. Make each tap deliberate and visible.
- **Take 3: Loop playing.** Show the circle rotating and sounds firing. Let it play for at least 5 seconds so the viewer "gets it."
- **Take 4: Draw a melody curve.** Switch mode (show the button press if you can), then draw a smooth ascending/descending wave.
- **Take 5: Move objects in space.** If your prototype supports grabbing/moving objects, capture this. If not, this becomes an AI-generated shot.

**Tips for clean VR footage:**
- Keep your head steady while recording — jerky VR footage is nauseating on a flat screen.
- Move the stylus slowly and deliberately. In VR it feels natural; on a 2D screen, fast movements blur.
- If your prototype's visuals are rough, that is fine — judges know this is a concept pitch, not a shipped product.
- Use a dark or neutral background in your VR scene so the glowing trails pop.

---

### C. RECORDING YOUR REAL-WORLD CAMERA FOOTAGE

You need two simple shots:

**Shot 1 (Beat 2): Putting on the headset**
- Frame: waist-up, you sitting or standing at a clean desk.
- On the desk: MX Ink stylus, Quest 3 headset. Nothing else distracting.
- Action: pick up the Quest 3, put it on, then pick up the MX Ink.
- Lighting: bright, natural light from a window or a desk lamp. Avoid overhead fluorescent.
- Camera: phone on a tripod or propped up. Horizontal (landscape) orientation. 1080p minimum.

**Shot 2 (Beat 6): Removing the headset**
- Same setup. Take off the Quest 3, look at the camera, natural smile.
- This humanizes the pitch. Judges connect with a face, not just a screen recording.

**Tip:** Wear a solid-color shirt (dark blue, black, or white). No logos, no busy patterns.

---

### D. AI-GENERATED FOOTAGE

Use AI video generation for the shots you cannot capture from your prototype:

**Shot list for AI generation:**

| Shot | Description | Prompt Direction |
|------|-------------|-----------------|
| **DAW montage (Beat 1)** | Close-ups of mouse clicking on a music production grid, fingers on a MIDI keyboard | "Close-up of hands clicking a mouse on a music production DAW software interface, dark bedroom studio, moody lighting, shallow depth of field" |
| **Galaxy hero shot (Beat 6)** | Wide view of a person surrounded by floating glowing orbs and light trails in VR | "Person standing in dark virtual space surrounded by floating glowing neon orbs, light trails forming circles and waves, futuristic music visualization, cinematic wide shot" |
| **Spatial mixing concept (Beat 5)** | Sound objects floating and moving in 3D space | "Glowing 3D objects floating in dark virtual reality space, some objects bright and close to camera, others dim and far, light trails connecting them, futuristic UI aesthetic" |

**Recommended AI video tools (in order of preference):**

1. **Google Veo 3.1** (via Google AI Studio or VideoFX) — Currently the best quality all-rounder. Generates clean, believable shots. Best for the "galaxy hero shot." Free tier available through Google AI Studio.

2. **Runway Gen-4.5** (runway.com) — Excellent for cinematic shots. Accepts an image as a starting point, so you can feed it a screenshot from your Quest prototype and it will enhance/animate it. Has a free trial with limited credits.

3. **Kling AI** (klingai.com) — Good for realistic human motion if you need a synthetic shot of someone using a headset. Free tier available.

4. **Pika** (pika.art) — Good for stylized, shorter clips (3–4 seconds). Free tier. Works well for abstract/concept visuals like the sound-object galaxy.

**Workflow for AI shots:**

1. Write your prompt (use the examples above as starting points).
2. Generate 3–4 variations per shot.
3. Pick the best one.
4. Each AI clip only needs to be 2–4 seconds long — you are cutting fast.
5. If the AI clip has artifacts or weird details, it is fine. At 2 seconds in a fast montage, the viewer will not notice.

**Pro tip — image-to-video:** If you can take a screenshot of your Quest prototype (even a rough one), feed that image to Runway Gen-4.5 as a starting frame and ask it to "animate" the scene. This creates continuity between your real footage and the AI-enhanced shots.

### E. FREE MUSIC (NO COPYRIGHT)

You need one background track — something electronic, forward-moving, builds energy, roughly 60 seconds.

**Best free sources:**

| Source | URL | What to Search | License |
|--------|-----|----------------|---------|
| **Pixabay Music** | [pixabay.com/music](https://pixabay.com/music/) | "electronic technology" or "future bass" or "ambient tech" | Pixabay Content License — free for commercial use, no attribution required |
| **Uppbeat** | [uppbeat.io](https://uppbeat.io/) | "technology" or "innovation" or "electronic chill" | Free tier — credit Uppbeat in your video description (just a text link) |
| **Bensound** | [bensound.com](https://www.bensound.com/) | Browse "Electronic" or "Corporate/Pop" categories | Free with attribution in video description |

**What kind of track to pick:**

- Genre: lo-fi electronic, ambient tech, or future bass.
- Tempo: medium (100–120 BPM). Not too fast — this is a pitch, not a gaming montage.
- Energy curve: starts calm/atmospheric (Beats 1–2), builds slightly in the middle (Beats 3–5), resolves at the end (Beat 6). Many "technology" tracks on Pixabay naturally follow this arc.
- Duration: find something at least 60 seconds. You can fade out at the end.
- **Avoid:** anything with vocals, heavy drops, or aggressive bass. You want the voiceover to sit clearly on top.

**Specific Pixabay track suggestions to start with:**
- Search "technology ambient" and sort by Most Popular. The top results are battle-tested by thousands of video creators.
- Search "innovation" — many results have the calm-to-uplifting arc you need.

**Download the track as MP3 or WAV. WAV is better quality for editing.**

### F. VOICEOVER RECORDING

Record your own voice. Judges connect with a real person, not an AI voice.

**Setup:**
- Use your phone's Voice Memos app or the free [Audacity](https://www.audacityteam.org/) app on your Mac.
- Record in a quiet room. Close windows, turn off fans/AC.
- Hold the phone 6–8 inches from your mouth, slightly to the side (not directly in front — reduces plosives like "p" and "b" sounds).
- Alternatively, use your AirPods as a microphone — surprisingly decent quality.

**Delivery tips:**
- Speak slightly slower than feels natural. Nerves speed you up.
- Pause between beats (the 6 sections). This gives you natural edit points.
- Smile while you speak — it genuinely changes the tone of your voice.
- Record the full script 3 times. Pick the best take for each section and cut them together.

**Script for recording (clean version, no stage directions):**

> "Music production has been stuck on a flat screen for decades. Click. Drag. Zoom in. Repeat.
>
> What if you could step inside the music... and build it with your hands?
>
> Draw a loop in the air. Place your beats. The circle rotates — and your rhythm comes alive. Pressure on the stylus controls how hard each hit lands.
>
> Switch modes with one click. Now draw a melody — height is pitch, pressure is volume. Tilt the stylus to bend notes like a guitar string.
>
> Every sound is an object you can touch. Move it closer — louder. Further — quieter. Paint effects in real time. This is mixing as spatial sculpture.
>
> RhythmForge VR. Create music with motion — powered by Logitech MX Ink."

**Word count: ~115 words. At a calm pace (~130 words/minute), this fills about 53 seconds — leaving 5–7 seconds for visual breathing room and the end card.**

### G. EDITING IN FINAL CUT PRO

**Project setup:**
1. Open Final Cut Pro → New Project.
2. Resolution: **1920×1080** (1080p). Frame rate: **30fps.**
3. Project name: `RhythmForgeVR_Pitch_v1`

**Import your assets:**
- All Quest 3 screen recordings
- Camera footage (headset on/off)
- AI-generated clips
- Background music track (WAV or MP3)
- Voiceover recording(s)

**Timeline structure:**

```
AUDIO TRACK 1 (top):    [——— Voiceover ———————————————————————————]
AUDIO TRACK 2 (bottom): [——— Background Music (lower volume) ——————]
VIDEO TRACK:             [B1][B2][——B3——][——B4——][——B5——][B6][END]
                          ↑    ↑    ↑       ↑       ↑     ↑    ↑
                         DAW  Real  Quest   Quest  AI+Q  Real  Card
                         montage    capture capture       camera
```

**Step-by-step editing workflow:**

**Step 1: Lay down the voiceover first.**
- Place your best voiceover take on the timeline.
- Use the Blade tool (B) to cut it into 6 segments (one per Beat).
- Space them out to approximately match the timings in the script.
- This is your "backbone" — everything else syncs to the voice.

**Step 2: Drop in the music track.**
- Place the background music on a lower audio track.
- Lower its volume to about **-15dB to -18dB** (the voice should always be clearly dominant).
- You can do this by selecting the music clip and adjusting the volume slider in the Inspector, or by dragging the horizontal volume line down on the clip in the timeline.

**Step 3: Cut and place video clips.**
- For each Beat, trim your footage to match the voiceover timing.
- Use keyboard shortcut **I** (set In point) and **O** (set Out point) in the preview window to select just the portion you need, then drag it to the timeline.

**Step 4: Add transitions.**
- Between Beat 1 and Beat 2: **hard cut to black** (add a 0.5s black slug — Generator → Elements → Custom in Final Cut Pro).
- Between all other beats: **simple cross-dissolve** (select the edit point, press Cmd+T). Keep dissolves short — 0.3 to 0.5 seconds.
- Do NOT use fancy transitions (star wipes, etc.). Clean cuts and dissolves only.

**Step 5: Add text overlays.**
- For feature labels ("Rhythm Canvas", "Melody Sculptor", "Spatial Mixing"): use the **Basic Lower Third** title (Edit → Connect Title → Basic Lower Third). White text, no background box, bottom-left corner. Small font — the footage is the star.
- For the product name reveal at 0:13: use a **Basic Title**, centered, white text, font size ~72. Fade in over 0.5s.
- For the end card: create a simple slide using Keynote or Canva (black background, white text, the text from the script). Export as PNG, import into Final Cut, place as last clip for 3 seconds.

**Step 6: Color and polish.**
- Add a slight color grade to your camera footage to match the mood. In Final Cut: select the clip → Color Inspector → choose "Warm" or "Neutral" from the preset dropdown. Subtle is better.
- For Quest 3 screen recordings: usually leave as-is. The VR visuals should look "VR-native."

**Step 7: Audio mixing.**
- Select the voiceover → Inspector → Audio → add the **Channel EQ** effect. Boost slightly around 3kHz for clarity. Cut below 80Hz to remove rumble.
- Make sure the music dips slightly during voiceover. You can do this manually with keyframes: Option-click on the volume line of the music track to add keyframe points, then dip the volume down during voice and bring it up during visual-only moments.
- Or use Final Cut's built-in **Audio Analysis** → **Loudness** to auto-level.

**Step 8: Export.**
- File → Share → Apple Devices 1080p.
- Or: File → Share → Master File (for highest quality), then upload.
- Format: H.264. Resolution: 1920×1080. Frame rate: 30fps.
- Target file size: under 100MB (for quick YouTube upload).

### K. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Going over 60 seconds.** Judges are told they don't have to watch past 1 minute. If your best content is at 0:55, they'll see it. If it's at 1:10, they might not.

2. **Too much talking, not enough showing.** The VR footage IS your pitch. The voiceover just guides the viewer's attention. If you have to choose between more words or more demo footage, always choose demo footage.

3. **Copyrighted music.** Even a 5-second clip of a popular song can get your video flagged or taken down. Stick to Pixabay/Uppbeat/Bensound.

4. **Poor audio levels.** If the music drowns out your voice, judges will skip ahead. Voice should always be clearly audible above the music. Test with earbuds AND laptop speakers before uploading.

5. **Apologizing for the prototype.** Never say "this is just a rough prototype" or "we didn't have time to finish." Show what works confidently. The judges know it is a concept pitch.
