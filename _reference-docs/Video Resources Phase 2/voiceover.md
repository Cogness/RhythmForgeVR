**Cinematic Voiceover Script**

Imagine creating music not by clicking on a screen, but by drawing it in the air around you.

This is RhythmForge VR, a spatial music creation experience for Meta Quest, built for the Logitech MX Ink stylus.

RhythmForge begins with a simple idea: music can be physical. A curve can become a melody. A gesture can become rhythm. A drawing can become structure. Instead of learning a complicated digital audio workstation, the user steps into an immersive space and starts composing with motion.

At the heart of the experience is a guided five-phase workflow.

First comes Harmony, where the user sketches the musical foundation of the piece. Then Melody, adding a lead line that follows the harmonic flow. Groove reshapes the rhythm and feel. Bass locks in the low-end support. And finally, Percussion builds the beat and completes the sequence.

Each phase is represented clearly in the interface, so the process feels intuitive and visual. You always know what layer you are creating, what is already complete, and what still needs to be explored. The goal is to make composition feel inviting, even for someone with no formal music training.

The MX Ink stylus is what makes this experience feel natural.

To create, the user draws directly in 3D space using pressure-sensitive input from the stylus tip or the middle pressure input on MX Ink. The stroke is captured as a living gesture, not just as a line. Its shape, direction, balance, and movement are analyzed by the system and translated into musical behavior.

When the stroke is complete, a commit card appears in front of the user. From there, the interaction stays simple and immediate. Press the front button to confirm the shape. Press the back button to cancel and try again.

This makes the workflow feel fluid: draw, listen, refine, continue.

What is powerful about RhythmForge is that it does not simply record shapes. It interprets them musically. The system keeps the result coherent, structured, and expressive, so the user can focus on creativity rather than technical complexity. Every new stroke adds another meaningful layer to the composition.

And then comes the most satisfying moment.

Once Harmony, Melody, Groove, Bass, and Percussion are in place, the user plays the full piece and hears a complete musical sequence generated from their own gestures in space.

What began as motion becomes music.

RhythmForge VR transforms the Logitech MX Ink from a drawing tool into a composition instrument, and opens a new way of thinking about creativity in mixed reality: more intuitive, more embodied, and more human.
